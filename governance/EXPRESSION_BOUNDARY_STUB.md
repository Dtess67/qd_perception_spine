# EXPRESSION_BOUNDARY_STUB

This is a Phase I constitutional artifact. It does not define the full expression contract. It defines the minimum boundary rules that must govern expression behavior before a full downstream expression layer is designed and ratified.

## Scope and Role in Phase I

- This document governs expression containment, not final expression architecture.
- It exists so that language output does not silently reshape cognition before the formal expression contract is written.
- It is an early boundary stub, not the full Phase VII expression contract.
- It applies to any human-readable output surface that attempts to speak for QD’s internal state.

## Core Boundary Decision

**Expression is downstream of cognition and must not become an epistemic override path.**

Stated more plainly:

- the mouth is not the mind
- polish is not proof
- narration is not judgment
- readability is not authority
- expression may translate internal state
- expression may not silently manufacture internal state

## Current Stub Classification

This document is a **containment stub**, not a full rendering contract.

That means:
- it is binding as an anti-drift boundary,
- it does not yet define every output class in full detail,
- it does not yet authorize broad natural-language elaboration,
- it exists to prevent premature expression-layer corruption.

## Boundary Rules

### E1. No reverse shaping from expression into cognition
Language output, wording preference, narrative convenience, or downstream presentation pressure must not alter the internal epistemic state merely because a cleaner answer is easier to say.

Expression may follow cognition.  
Expression may not silently revise cognition.

### E2. No invention of certainty
If the internal state is unresolved, conditional, degraded, limited, or HOLD-worthy, expression must not render it as resolved certainty.

Readable wording does not authorize stronger judgment than the underlying state earned.

### E3. No collapse of HOLD into absence, timeout, or filler
HOLD / Na must remain a real epistemic outcome in expression.

It must not be translated away as:
- missing output,
- evasive filler,
- generic apology,
- decorative hedging that hides the real state.

If the system is in HOLD, expression must preserve that fact honestly.

### E4. No semantic upgrade by eloquence
Style, confidence, fluency, emotional smoothness, or rhetorical strength must not upgrade a weak or bounded internal state into a stronger one.

A better sentence is not a better judgment.

### E5. No expression of states that are not traceable
An output class must be traceable back to a real internal state, bounded source, or governed decision surface.

If the expression cannot be traced back to something real in the architecture, it must not be treated as authoritative system meaning.

### E6. No silent substitution by external models
If an LLM or other external language surface is used for wording, that surface may assist with rendering but may not:
- invent supporting evidence,
- reinterpret the governing state silently,
- replace internal uncertainty with rhetorical smoothness,
- upgrade output class beyond what the internal state permits.

### E7. No human-preference backflow into claimed neutral substrate
Human-readable expression choices may exist downstream, but they must not flow backward into neutral substrate claims, detector behavior, or constitutional interpretation merely because a human finds one wording more intuitive.

### E8. No unmarked mixture of observation and decoration
If expression contains both traceable state-rendering and optional explanatory gloss, those functions must remain distinguishable.

The system must not blur:
- what the architecture actually knows,
- what the renderer is adding for readability,
- what remains unknown or bounded.

### E9. No degradation concealment
If output quality is limited by degraded substrate, missing continuity, unavailable evidence, fallback mode, or unresolved contradiction, expression must not hide that limitation.

Limitation that affects judgment must remain visible in some honest form.

### E10. No canonical authority from the stub alone
This stub prevents bad expression behavior.  
It does not, by itself, define the final output taxonomy, full rendering map, or full traceability contract.

Those belong to the later formal expression contract.

## Minimum Expression Duties Under This Stub

Until the full contract exists, any expression layer that speaks for QD must preserve at least the following:

1. whether the system is resolved, conditional, limited, degraded, or HOLD
2. whether the output is bounded by missing or weak evidence
3. whether the wording is traceable to a real internal state
4. whether the renderer is assisting rather than deciding
5. whether any limitation materially affects confidence or closure

If these are not preserved, the expression behavior violates this stub.

## Allowed Behavior Under This Stub

The following are allowed, if they do not violate the boundary rules above:

- translating a traceable internal state into readable language
- simplifying wording for human comprehension
- making uncertainty legible rather than opaque
- preserving HOLD in human-readable form
- exposing limitation, degradation, or boundedness honestly
- using downstream rendering help so long as it does not become epistemic authority

## Not Yet Decided Here

This stub does **not** yet decide:

- the final complete output-class mapping
- the final syntax or formatting of expression surfaces
- the full contract between internal states and LLM-rendered text
- the final explanation policy for every output class
- the complete traceability schema for future expression layers

Those belong to the later full expression contract, not this Phase I containment artifact.

## What Must Not Be Confused

- readable does **not** mean true
- fluent does **not** mean earned
- confident does **not** mean resolved
- detailed does **not** mean grounded
- polite does **not** mean honest
- expressive does **not** mean authoritative

## Relationship to Later Work

This document exists so Phase I can close without leaving expression as an ungoverned hole.

Later Phase VII work may expand this into a full expression contract, but that later contract must remain consistent with this stub’s core boundary:

**expression is downstream and may not silently become the source of belief, closure, or authority.**

## Pressure-Test Readiness

This document is pressure-test-ready if the following can all be checked:

1. it prevents language-model convenience from becoming epistemic override
2. it preserves HOLD / Na as a real expressive outcome
3. it prevents fluent overclaiming
4. it keeps traceability as a hard requirement
5. it stays narrow enough to remain a stub rather than pretending to be the full contract

## Non-Goals / Non-Claims

- This document does not claim the final expression contract is complete.
- This document does not ban human-readable rendering.
- This document does not ban future LLM-assisted expression.
- This document does not define final wording templates.
- This document does not decide the full speech architecture.

It exists to answer one immediate constitutional need:

**before a full expression contract exists, what must expression be forbidden from doing?**
